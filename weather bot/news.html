Record-Breaking Heatwave in Japan

Record-Breaking Heatwave: Japan is currently sweltering under its worst heatwave since records began in 1875 1. Tokyo recently charted temperatures above 35°C for a fifth straight day, marking the worst documented streak of hot weather in June 2. In the city of Isesaki, located northwest of Tokyo, a record-breaking temperature of 40.2°C was recorded—the highest ever for June in Japan 2. Calls for Energy Conservation: The intense heat has led to official warnings of a looming power shortage. Authorities have urged people to conserve energy by turning off unused lights and using air conditioning judiciously 3. Despite the need to save energy, the government advises using air conditioning to prevent heatstroke cases, especially among vulnerable populations 1. Climate Change Impact: Heatwaves have become more frequent, intense, and prolonged due to human-induced climate change. The world has already warmed by about 1.1°C since the industrial era began, and temperatures will continue to rise unless significant emissions cuts are made globally
